AMARKANTAK

Amarkantak is known as the Teerthraj- the king of pilgrimages.It is the meeting point of Vindhya,Maikal and Satpura ranges. The Narmada, one of the holiest rivers of India,originates here.The forests surrounding Amarkantak contain a rich variety of medicinal plants.

BANDHAVGARH

Bandhavgarh in Madhya Pradesh is a place of legendary significance.It is believed that the Bandhavgarh Fort was gifted to Lakshmana by his older brother Lord Ram to keep a watch on Lanka.All the white tigers of the world trace their roots to Bandhavgarh.Boasts of the highest density of royal bengal tigers in the world.

Bhedaghat

It is a mesmerising experience to witness the marble rocks adjoining the graceful Narmada River in Bhedaghat,Jabalpur. Egg shells of dinosaurs have been found at a number of ghats in the region.The best time to visit the falls is during the monsoon.we can visit the beautiful 10th century chaunsath yogini temple,dedicated to goddess durga.

Tawa

The raw wilderness and absolute tranquility of Tawa dam and Madai reserve forest attracts tourists in good numbers. Located in the Hoshangabad district of Madhya pradesh, are the hot spots in monsoon.This place offers a scenic escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Hanuwantiya

Situated at the bank of asia's second largest reservoir,the Indira Sagar Lake.Hanuwantiya also hosts India's only and largest water carnival,Jal Mahotsav every year.This grand event is a paradise for adventure seekers,art entusiasts,foodies and tired of the urban chaos people.

KHAJURAHO

The common date palm -Khajura- has given Khajuraho its name.The famous erotic sculptures of Khajuraho account for only 10% of all sculptures.Khajuraho is an ancient city for its magnificient temples and intricate sculptures.It holds the spellbinding historical tales and architectural grandeurs.

MANDSAUR

Mandsaur is believed to be the home town of mandodari,wife of Ravana.known for its tresure of archaeological sites.A remarkable place to see in Mandsaur is the Pashupatinath Temple.The famous Pashupatinath Temple attracts devotees in huge numbers every year.
